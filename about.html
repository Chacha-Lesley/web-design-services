Origin Story

From bootcamp to building real things

There's a particular kind of satisfaction in seeing something you built go live — watching a business owner's face when they first see their vision rendered in pixels. That feeling is why I do this.

I graduated from PLP's software engineering bootcamp and quickly moved from code exercises to real client sites. Since then, I've worked with startups finding their footing, NGOs expanding their reach, churches connecting with congregations, and individuals building their personal brands.

My approach is straightforward: I listen first, design second. Every site I build starts with understanding what you're trying to accomplish — not just what you think you need. Then I bring the creativity, the strategy, and the technical skill to make it happen fast.

Great design, to me, isn't about complexity. It's about clarity. A visitor should understand exactly what you do and why it matters within seconds of landing on your page. That's the standard I hold myself to.

What I Stand For

Core values

01

Simplicity

Elegance is subtractive. The best websites remove everything that isn't absolutely essential, leaving only what drives action and communicates value. I design with clarity as the north star.

02

Sustainability

Clean code, lightweight pages, and honest pricing — I build things that last and won't trap you in dependency cycles. Your site should be something you own and can grow.

03

Inclusivity

Every person who lands on your site deserves a great experience — regardless of device, connection speed, or ability. Accessibility isn't optional; it's foundational to good design.
